# SAFe Advanced Scrum Master (SASM)

*The next step after SSM for a Scrum Master doing the job full-time inside a SAFe enterprise — moving from facilitating one team's events to facilitating an entire Agile Release Train, with flow and Kanban thinking layered on top.*

## Who It's For

SASM is for Scrum Masters already operating inside a real SAFe implementation who have run enough Program Increments to have hit the edges of what [SSM](safe-scrum-master.md) covered. SSM teaches you to facilitate your own team's events and participate in ART-level ceremonies; SASM assumes you've done that for a while and are ready to take on more of the ART-level facilitation itself — running Scrum of Scrums well instead of just attending it, contributing meaningfully to designing the Inspect & Adapt workshop, and coaching other teams' Scrum Masters rather than only your own team. It is a poor fit for anyone who hasn't yet lived through a few real PIs; without that grounding, the ART-level material has nothing concrete to attach to.

## Format & Logistics

Course providers commonly recommend or require an active [SSM](safe-scrum-master.md) plus genuine hands-on ART experience before enrolling in SASM, though the exact prerequisite policy is set by Scaled Agile and best confirmed on their site before you register, since prerequisite requirements for SAFe certifications have shifted over time. The course itself runs two days, delivered by an SPC from licensed Scaled Agile content, following the same overall shape as SSM: exam access included with course registration, taken online through Scaled Agile's proctored exam platform within a set window after the course.

Exam format is broadly similar to SSM's — historically in the range of 45 multiple-choice questions with a passing score around 73% — and, as with SSM, treat those figures as approximate and verify current specifications with Scaled Agile before your exam date. Course pricing sits in a comparable band to SSM, commonly discussed around $1,000–$1,400 USD depending on provider and region; confirm current pricing directly. Renewal follows the same SAFe pattern as SSM: annual, tied to an active SAFe Community Platform membership and fee — though note SASM sits in Scaled Agile's "SAFe Advanced" membership tier (commonly cited around $295/year), a step above SSM's Foundational tier, and Scaled Agile has introduced PDU/SEU-style continuing-education elements alongside the fee for at least some levels. **Verify current membership tier pricing and any continuing-education requirement on scaledagile.com.** The certification lapses if not renewed.

## What It Actually Tests

SASM shifts the lens from single-team facilitation to ART-level facilitation and coaching at scale. Expect course content on facilitating Program Increment Planning itself rather than just participating in it — designing the two-day agenda, managing cross-team dependency conversations in the room, and handling the breakout-and-reconverge structure that makes PI Planning work across a full train. It also covers running effective Scrum of Scrums and ART sync, designing a System Demo and Inspect & Adapt workshop that actually surfaces systemic problems rather than producing theater, and coaching other Scrum Masters on the train rather than just your own team's dynamics.

A significant and distinctive part of the curriculum leans into flow and Kanban topics: visualizing cross-team dependencies, managing Work in Process (WIP) limits at ART level, and using flow metrics to understand how work is actually moving across a full train rather than relying on any single team's Sprint-level view. This is SAFe's applied, train-scale version of ideas this guide covers in general form in [Optimizing Flow](../../03-professional-scrum-competencies/06-optimizing-flow.md) — SASM teaches you to apply that thinking specifically inside SAFe's ART structure.

## Study Plan

Come into the course with real PI cycles already behind you. The material assumes SSM-level vocabulary — ART, PI, Iteration, WSJF — is already second nature and spends its actual class time on ART-level facilitation technique and flow thinking rather than re-teaching PI Planning basics; if you're still learning that vocabulary, the course will move past you.

Read [Optimizing Flow](../../03-professional-scrum-competencies/06-optimizing-flow.md) before the course rather than after. SASM introduces flow and Kanban concepts — WIP limits, cumulative flow, flow-based metrics — as SAFe-flavored material, and it lands far better if you already have the underlying concepts from general flow theory in hand rather than encountering WIP limits for the first time inside a SAFe-branded slide deck. The course builds on that foundation; it doesn't build the foundation itself.

Bring real ART-level problems from your own organization into the room: a chronic dependency conflict between two teams that resurfaces every PI, a Scrum of Scrums that has quietly become a status meeting nobody finds useful, a PI Planning event that reliably runs over schedule. SASM is a practitioner course, and its exercises are built to be worked against real situations — the value is almost entirely in applying the material to your own train, not in passing the multiple-choice exam that follows.

## Common Failure Points

The most common failure mirrors A-CSM's: enrolling without the real ART experience the course assumes. Someone who holds SSM but has only ever attended PI Planning as a participant, never helped design or run one, will find the ART-facilitation content abstract rather than actionable — the course teaches you to run something you haven't yet seen the inside of well enough to improve on.

The second failure is treating SASM's flow and Kanban content as a substitute for actually learning flow theory in depth. The course introduces the concepts at the depth SAFe needs them, not the depth a dedicated Kanban practice would build. Scrum Masters who want to genuinely master flow thinking should treat [Optimizing Flow](../../03-professional-scrum-competencies/06-optimizing-flow.md) as the real primer and SASM as SAFe's applied, ART-specific gloss on top of it — useful and legitimate, but not a replacement for the deeper material if flow is going to be a real strength rather than a certification talking point.

Finally, SASM is exposed to the same badge-collection trap as SSM, just one level up: passing through the annual renewal treadmill — course, exam, membership fee, repeat — without the underlying ART-facilitation and coaching-at-scale skill actually deepening year over year. The credential is meant to represent someone who has genuinely grown into running a train's coordination, not just someone who has attended a second SAFe course after the first one.
